Introduction

This page provides an overview of my academic research and personal projects. It highlights my work as a PhD candidate in soft matter physics, as well as various coding and data science projects I have undertaken.

Bio

I am a PhD candidate at Dalhousie University, specializing in soft matter physics. My research focuses on the mechanics of liquid bridges and fiber formation in protein solutions. Beyond my academic work, I have a strong interest in coding, gaming, and music.

Research

My research centers on soft matter physics, with a particular focus on the fluid dynamics of complex viscous solutions and liquid bridge mechanics. I investigate how solvent evaporation, pull speed, and protein concentration influence fiber formation in protein solutions such as zein. My work involves high-speed imaging, mathematical modeling, and machine learning techniques to optimize fiber production. Additionally, I explore rheology, diffusion-driven skin formation, non-Newtonian fluid behavior, and contact drawing methods.

Projects

I developed a Python package for LaTeX automation and an application that employs machine learning to predict Dota 2 match outcomes. I have also conducted diffusion modeling research, focusing on anisotropic solvent evaporation in fiber formation.
